Illegally Alive

"...your very existence is aginst the law..."

Describes the experience of being born into a society where your very existence is against the law and seen as a threat to the rules that uphold injustice. As Trevor Noah, the first moment of his life was forced to live in hiding, to be cautious, and to fight for his right to simply exist. He was considered "illegally alive" because, under South Africa's apartheid laws, relationships between black and white people were illegal, and children born from such uniions were not supposed to exist. His life was a crime not because of what he did , but because of who he was.

"Love should be about accepting and uplifting someone as they are, not reshaping them into a version that fits traditional norms and not silencing them. True love sets people free. It never cages them."

The quote from Born a Crime "Abel wanted a traditional marriage with a traditional wife...but he never fell in love with subservient women..'He only wants a woman who is free because his dream is to put her in a cage.'"powerfully reveals a disturbing truth about certain relationship dynamics, loving someone for their strength while secretly wanting to break that strength and controling them.

Abel is drawn to Patricia because of her independence, intelligence, and strong will. She defies the system, raises her son alone, and builds a life with her own hands, which was not expected and allowed in Abel's culture. This contradiction still happens in many relationships today.

Society often praises strong and empowered woman, but in practice, some men feel threatened by that strength to control it. I have seen women who are confident and successful are admired at first, but later pressured to give up their careers and dreams to fit a more traditional role. Even celebrities like Michelle Obama have faced criticism for being too outspoken as a wife, showing how society still struggles with powerful woman.

This quote mentions the tension between gender roles and personal expectations. Abel wants a traditional wife, obedient, quiet and dependent, but he falls in love with a woman who is none of those things. His love becomes violent, not because Patricia changes, but because he can't control her. This reflects a wider issue where some relationships are built on power rather than partnership. Some men thinks women are naturally under men's power. For that they want to control and show them their power. They don't want them to use their brain, to communicate and share their ideas with anyone. They just want them to do house chores and raise their kids.
